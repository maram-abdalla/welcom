Smart Hand Gloves

Imagine if blind people could see the world not with their eyes, but with technology! This research introduces a smart glove that turns obstacles and hazards into audio alerts, giving users the freedom to navigate with unprecedented confidence and safety.

background research:

Blind individuals face significant mobility challenges due to the limitations of traditional aids. White canes detect only nearby obstacles, often leaving users vulnerable to unexpected hazards. Guide dogs, despite their benefits, are inaccessible to most, as only 2-8% of blind individuals worldwide use them due to high training costs exceeding $50,000 and the need for ongoing care. Relying on human assistance, while helpful, limits personal independence. These challenges make daily navigation difficult, highlighting the need for a more accessible and efficient solution. Could technology unlock a new way of 'seeing' for the blind, enhancing their independence and mobility?

Introduction

help blind people

Blindness is a permanent condition that requires constant care to avoid injuries and exposure to others. Blind people must rely on themselves and technology to navigate their environment safely. Smart hand gloves can help them appreciate obstacles without knowing their distance, allowing them to move easily and safely without relying on others.

Reduce Accidents

Research Mobility-Related Accidents Experienced by Doctor of Philosophy Roberto and Doctor of Philosophy Sri found that 92% of participants experienced head-level accidents more than once a month due to default white walking sticks and guide dogs failing to warn visually impaired individuals. Smart hand gloves for blind people can help them move around indoors, reducing the workload for those who assist the blind.

Project Targets

• Low price: affordable development thus far and the material is available anywhere.
• Safety: The smart hand gloves do not contain any harmful substances. RoHS (Reduction of Hazardous Substances) = Yes.
• High efficiency: Helps to ease the navigation process for the needy, reducing the risk of walking for blind people.
• Clean power source: We use rechargeable batteries.

Data analysis

96.25% accuracy in detecting obstacles and giving audio alerts. Fast response and accurate environmental analysis of temperature and thermal hazards. High accuracy in obstacle detection: The sensors successfully recognize obstacles in different directions (right, left, front, above, below). Immediate response: Audio alerts are issued as soon as an obstacle is detected, giving the user enough time to change their course. Detection of thermal hazards: The flame sensor efficiently identifies the presence of fire, helping to avoid dangerous situations. Providing accurate environmental data: The temperature sensor provides the user with precise information about the surrounding temperature, enabling them to make appropriate decisions while navigating.
